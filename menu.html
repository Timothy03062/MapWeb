Aim

The aim of the data analysis is to observe a relationship between the amount of motor vehicles a household may have to a household's weekly median income.

Analysis

It has been observed at there has been a significant increase in coastal areas of NSW in households with two motor vehicles, and the inner west of Sydney. For weekly income, there also seems to be strong median income increases within Sydney, eastward regions and small pockets interstate in 2016 compared to 2011. Although there are varying circumstances, the increase in Sydney seems to match along prominent public transport lines and the city's "Three City" concept.


Motor Vehicles

The growth of households possessing with two motor vehicles has been small in NSW overall between 2011 and 2016 respectively, primarily in regional NSW. The general trend has shown a strong increases in middle and eastern(coastal) NSW, with significant increases of in areas such as metropolitan Sydney, Newcastle, Wagga Wagga.

Comparison

The 'latte line' is an imaginary boundary that splits Sydney down class and economic lines, running from the airport north-west through Parramatta. Both data sets relate to the "latte line" as the North-Western region of Sydney appears to be experiencing more development and growth compared to other regions. The divide seems to be more clear between 2011 and 2016 in income growth in concentrated regions like Parramatta and Epping compared to thier sourthern counterparts. In terms of motor vehicles, although the divide is slight and mostly evenly spread out, the north still has more households with two vehicles when comparing data from 2011 and 2016.

This is significant to the relationship between income and vehicles, as it proves that households with more income are more likely to purchase more motor vehicles. What it also signifies is the labour mobility of people.Although typically the north is more "prosperous" than the south, individuals and families could move to less costly areas of Sydney to save costs and the rise in income potentially allows households to purchase more vehicles and travel further distances to work.



The NSW government has proposed a "Three City" development plan, stretching from Sydney CBD, Parramatta and Badgerys Creek. It can be seen within metropolitan Sydney from visualisation of income and vehicles, that between 2011, 2016 and recent statistics that most growth and developement is happening around these indicated city concentrations and aligns with the proposal. This is most likely because of both government and business future investment and development projections.
